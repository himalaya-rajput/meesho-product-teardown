# 🛍️ Meesho Product Teardown

> **Research-driven Product Management Case Study on Meesho's Monetization Strategy**

This project explores how Meesho can improve monetization without compromising its affordability-first positioning.

Using primary research, user interviews, competitive benchmarking, and product strategy frameworks, our team identified key trust gaps in the customer journey and designed three monetization features supported by an interactive prototype, revenue guesstimates, and a product metrics framework.

## 🔍 Research Approach

To ensure recommendations were backed by evidence rather than assumptions, we combined qualitative and quantitative research.

### Primary Research
- 63 survey responses
- 9 user interviews
- Students, professionals, homemakers and retired users

### Secondary Research
- Meesho DRHP
- Industry reports
- Public customer reviews
- Competitor benchmarking

### Deliverables
- User Personas

## 💡 Key Insights

Research consistently pointed toward one conclusion:

> **Users are willing to pay for certainty—not speed.**

Major findings:

- Lowest price remains the biggest purchase driver.
- Trust influences buying decisions more than delivery speed.
- Unexpected delivery charges increase cart abandonment.
- Users showed willingness to pay ₹20–50 if delivery promises were guaranteed.

## 🚀 Proposed Solutions

| Feature | Goal |
|---------|------|
| Smart Bundle Add-ons | Increase Average Order Value |
| Paid Express Delivery | Generate per-order revenue |
| Meesho Lite | Build recurring subscription revenue |

## 🎨 Interactive Prototype

To validate the proposed checkout experience, we designed an interactive prototype demonstrating:

- Bundle recommendations
- Express delivery opt-in
- Premium packaging
- Meesho Lite subscription

The prototype helped evaluate whether users perceived these premium features as valuable without disrupting the existing buying experience.
